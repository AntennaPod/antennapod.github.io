As an open-source project, AntennaPod doesn't exist in a vacuum. We rely on a wider ecosystem of open-source tools to keep our project running smoothly. And as many of you know, an important piece of our infrastructure is Weblate, the platform where our global community [translates](https://antennapod.org/contribute/translate) AntennaPod into over 40 languages.

When we migrated our app translations from Transifex to Weblate in 2024, our goal was to bring the localisation of the whole project (website and app) under one common open-source roof. But as we settled into our new home, we hit a few technical walls. Today, we are proud to share how we used your generous donations to not only break down those walls for AntennaPod, but to make Weblate better for the entire open-source community.

## Turning donations into upstream development

Thanks to everyone who has been chipping in to our OpenCollective, AntennaPod is in a solid financial position. After [consulting our community](https://forum.antennapod.org/t/proposal-donation-to-weblate/5710) about making a donation to Weblate, in 2025 we approached the Weblate team about it and discussed the biggest friction points we were facing. That got the ball rolling and led to us agreeing to sponsor the work to address those issues. After meeting with the Weblate team at [FOSDEM 2026](https://antennapod.org/blog/2026/02/fosdem), we realized that some of our requests required deep, structural changes to Weblate's architecture. To make that happen, our community approved a sponsorship of almost €4,000 to fund Weblate's work on these changes.

## What we helped build (and why it matters)

Following the release of [Weblate 2026.6](https://weblate.org/en/news/archive/20266-different-picture/) earlier this month, all the features we sponsored are available to Weblate users and Weblate received our sponsorship. This is what we funded, from largest to smallest sponsorship size:

- *[Some component settings should be settable on the project and inherited](https://github.com/WeblateOrg/weblate/issues/10897#issuecomment-3831241112)*, which also addressed our need for a *[Contributor agreement at project level](https://github.com/WeblateOrg/weblate/issues/14231)*
- *[Allow setting language permission at user level](https://github.com/weblateOrg/weblate/issues/14233)*
- *[Enable add-ons at category level](https://github.com/weblateOrg/weblate/issues/17858)*
- *[‘Translate’ button at category landing page](https://github.com/weblateOrg/weblate/issues/11198)*, which also addressed our need to *[Add multiple Markdown files (or folder) as single component](https://github.com/WeblateOrg/weblate/issues/14237)*. This was not implemented as such, but instead the languages page now opens by default when opening a category, i.e. a group of components.
- *[Add languages at category level](https://github.com/weblateOrg/weblate/issues/17852)*
- *[Expose ‘add comment’ API endpoint](https://github.com/weblateOrg/weblate/issues/14232)*
- *[Add separate ‘post announcements’ permission](https://github.com/weblateOrg/weblate/issues/8974)*

## A win-win for the ecosystem

For AntennaPod's localisation, these improvements mean a significantly reduced attack surface and a streamlined workflow. But the best part of this? Every other project using Weblate gets these features for free.
This is exactly what community-funded open source is supposed to look like: users supporting an app, and in turn that app contributing to the platforms it relies on.

Thank you to everyone who contributed, donated or helped AntennaPod in any other way. You made this happen!
