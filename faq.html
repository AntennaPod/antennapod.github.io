AntennaPod F.A.Q.

Getting started

How do I subscribe to a podcast?

Open the drawer by swiping from the left or tapping the burger icon on the top left corner. Go to Add Podcast. Now, you can search, browse suggestions or add a podcast by URL.

Alternatively, you can import an OPML file or click 'Advanced Search' to only check the iTunes, gpodder.net or fyyd database respectively. Importing RSS or Atom feeds, or URL schemes like pcast:// and itpc://, will also work.

Why can't manually reorder the queue?

If you can't move episodes around in the queue, this can be caused by either of the following:

  • 'Keep Sorted' is enabled. You can disable this by tapping the three dots in the top bar Sort.

General

First aid

  1. Uninstall Prestissimo:
    On Android 6.0+ (Marshmallow and newer), you can use Android's native mediaplayer for variable playback speed.
    On older devices, enable Sonic mediaplayer.
  2. Prefer MP3 feeds over other formats, especially MP4/AAC:
    We use Android's built-in audio decoders. A lot of devices are shipped with rather buggy MP4 decoders that lead to various playback issues and even native crashes (think Bluescreen).
    In our opinion, your best option is to subscribe to MP3 feeds whenever possible.

You made a spelling mistake

Translations are provided by the community. If you found a typo or would like to suggest a better translation, you can do that on Transifex.

How do I remove those ads?

Our builds are completely ad-free and open source! Get them at Google Play or F-Droid. You can even get the latest release on GitHub and build AntennaPod yourself!

Why are some downloaded episodes deleted?

AntennaPod has several mechanisms to make sure downloaded podcasts will not fill up your phone's memory. If you notice some downloaded episodes being deleted, have a look at these two settings:

  • Episode Cleanup (Settings Network Automatic Download). If Automatic Download needs space for new episodes, it will delete old episodes that are not in the queue and not marked as favorite. You can add a delay (x time after playback) or Episode . The setting 'Episode Cache' determines the maximum amount of downloaded episodes.
  • Auto Delete (Settings Storage). When enabled, episodes are deleted when playback completes. You can disable this option.

How do I keep my data when changing phone?

There are a few options to copy your data to your new phone, and each method copies a certain amount of data:

  • Save your subscriptions in an OPML file. You will loose any podcast- or episode-specific data (like if a specific episode has been played) and all app settings. To export your file, go to Settings Storage Import/Export. Be sure to click 'Send…' to save the file in another folder on your phone or somewhere else, because the original file will be deleted when you uninstall AntennaPod.
  • Sync your subscriptions via gpodder.net. This will save some podcast & episode data (e.g. playback position), but you will loose your favorites, queue, and app settings. To enable sync, go to Settings Integrations.
  • Export the AntennaPod database. This is experimental, and you should make sure you have the same version of AntennaPod on both the old and new phone. After importing all data is restored; you will only need to re-download episodes.
Playback

Episodes are playing way too fast!

Some Android 4.1 and 4.2 devices have problems identifying mono sound correctly. The best solution is to switch the media player from the Sonic to ExoPlayer in the Settings (search 'media player').

Playback stops/crashes constantly

Are you playing a MP4 file? If so, we could be dealing with a buggy MP4 decoder. Try subscribing to the MP3 feed instead.

You should also try to enable or disable the Sonic mediaplayer.

External storage

My SD card does not show up in the "Choose Data Folder" dialog

For your SD card to show up, Android must recognize the card as external storage. Either the card is not mounted correctly or the manufacturer or your smartphone configured the hardware incorrectly.

If you are running Android 6+ (Marshmallow or newer), you can try formatting the SD card as internal storage. Else, there is nothing we can do to fix this.

It isn't working...

If you are running Android 6+, make sure AntennaPod has sufficient permissions to the storage (Android settings Apps).
Flattr

Flattr settings are greyed out

You probably didn't install AntennaPod from the Google Play Store. Your build doesn't contain the necessary credentials for Flattr integration to work.

Project

How can I contribute?

AntennaPod is under active development by volunteers. You can contribute too, with code or with comment!

  • You speak any other language than English? Help us translate AntennaPod via Transifex
  • You can explain well? Help other users in our Google Group
  • You are good at breaking stuff? Become a Beta Tester
  • You can program in Java? Help test pull requests or submit new features or bug fixes on GitHub

How can I donate?

Thank you for considering a donation. The current developers decided to not accept donations. If you have some time to spare, we would warmly welcome your involvement in our community (see previous question).

Why is AntennaPod not on Store X or Y?

Unfortunately, we don't have the time to support any other store than Google Play and F-Droid.

How do I become a Beta Tester?

Every new version of AntennaPod is sent to our Beta Testers via the Google Play store, before it is shipped to the general public. It allows us to test updates on many different devices and Android versions, so we can catch bugs and user experience issues on time. If you have a critical eye and can explain what's going wrong, your help is very welcome!

Simply visit the AntennaPod listing in the Google Play store and go to "Become a beta tester" at the bottom of the page. Or go here if you're on a computer.

Can you update AntennaPod on F-Droid? It's not the latest version.

The F-Droid repository is not maintained by us, but by the people behind F-Droid. That means there's only so much we can do. But, you can always let us know by creating a ticket on GitHub, and we'll investigate it.
